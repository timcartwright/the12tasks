The 12 Tasks Challenge

The 12 Tasks Challenge is a real life travel adventure game where teams of two are challenged to complete cultural, fun, creative and adventurous tasks as they travel and explore the world.

The tasks are designed to challenge but also to immerse you into a country and its culture, providing unique experiences to remember and amazing stories to tell.

Each completed task will earn points for your team. The pair with the most points at the end of the game will win an invite to return and defend their 12 Tasks Crown.

FAQs

What's it all about?

The 12 Tasks Challenge is a real life travel adventure game.

It's been described as an exotic mix of treasure hunt, magical mystery tour, travel adventure and charity challenge. Your team will be given fun and challenging tasks which are designed to immerse you into the country and experience it in an new and unique way.

It is a game - with a winner and a prize - but it is not a race (although there may be a race within the game). We prefer to travel slow and deep, search out the less trodden tracks and give you time to appreciate people and places.

The Thailand Challenge kicks off in Bangkok with the opening night welcome party. For the next 12 days you will take part in a series of adventures, tasks and challenges in different parts of the country. On the last night is the celebration dinner where the winner is crowned and memories, food and drink are shared. The winning team will be invited back as our guests to defend their title.

Is it for me?

The 12 Tasks is for adventurous people who want to explore a country, its people, and its culture. You should enjoy being pushed outside your comfort zone and embrace the opportunity to deal with new and unexpected situations.

Although many aspects are organised for you - and we will be in country to assist you if needed - this is not a tour and you should be ready to travel independently in a foreign country.

It is not necessary to be competitive but it helps if you are trying to win!

There are 2 people in each team, so it's best if you enter together. If you really can't find a teammate then let us know and we might be able to pair you up with someone.

Where will we go?

A good question but sorry, we can't tell you! Part of the challenge is to respond to unexpected situations and places.

For the Thailand Challenge this is what we can say:

We will start in Bangkok, and get you back to Bangkok at the end. We like to get off the beaten track, travel & eat with locals, and take part in Thai festivals and events. For the rest, you'll have to wait and see.

Do we travel independently or in a group?

The amount of time you spend with the other teams is largely up to you. Some tasks are group tasks but these will generally be in bigger cities where you will also have the space to do your own thing if you want. In rural areas we try to avoid staying in groups which might spoil the interactions with the local people.

On the road, the route is designed with enough flexibility so that no two teams will follow the same course - unless they choose to. We will all be together for the welcome party, the final dinner and usually a rendezvous somewhere in the middle.

Where will we stay?

It depends where we are and what we are doing. In the city you will typically stay in a comfortable hotel, in a village you might find yourself in a homestay, or in the jungle it may be a tent: basically, wherever is appropiate for the location and task.

If we can stay in a unique and interesting place then we do. Wherever it is will be clean and you will be well looked after.

Is it a race?

It is not a race. There may be time based tasks within the game but we prefer to explore the depths rather than dash about on the surface.

Do I need to be fit?

It is not a physical challenge, but there are tasks which will require a reasonable level of fitness.

To give you an idea of the level of fitness required, you should be comfortable hiking up a mountain path or spending a few hours walking around under the tropical sun.

An easy and enjoyable way to get fit is by joining your local Parkrun

How do I win?

Each task is worth a number of points and the team finishing with the most points will win.

A successful team will be resourceful, creative and quick thinking, demonstrate stamina and curiosity, and be willing to throw themselves fully into the challenge.

What is the prize?

The winning team will be invited to return as our guests to defend their title - wherever The 12 Tasks Challenge is held (subject to a few t&c's).

What's included in the entry fee?

After you've completed the registration process, we will set your team up with your own 12 Tasks blog. You will use this (together with social media) to complete some of the tasks - as well for your own blogging pleasure!

On arrival in Bangkok, we will give you a welcome pack containing various goodies - including the all important local SIM card (with internet access) and the highly sought after 12 Tasks t-shirts.

During the game, most of your accommodation is provided, some of your meals, most of your transport in Thailand as well as the fees for your core tasks. We can't say exactly, as each game is unique, but on average you will need to find 2-3 nights accommodation by yourself as part of the game.

Also included is the opening party, final dinner, plus of course your Task Masters (that's us!) who will be with you in Thailand in person and reachable by phone throughout the game. All in all, your extra expenditure could be as little as Â£100 - but this is entirely up to you.

What do I need to bring with me?

Bring your smart phone (with camera, internet, GPS etc) - we will provide you with a local SIM. Bring a pair of trainers (sports shoes suitable for running/hiking), and a torch. Above all, pack light. You can buy anything extra you need in Bangkok and it's cheaper than back home.
